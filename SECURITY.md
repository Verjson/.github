# Security Policy

The verJSON team takes the security of our software and services seriously. We
appreciate the efforts of security researchers and the community in helping keep
our projects and users safe.

## Reporting a Vulnerability

**Please do not report security vulnerabilities through public GitHub issues,
discussions, or pull requests.**

Instead, report them privately using one of the following channels:

- **GitHub Security Advisories** (preferred): Open a private report via the
  **Security** tab of the affected repository, or directly at
  [github.com/Verjson/.github/security/advisories/new](https://github.com/Verjson/.github/security/advisories/new).
- **Email**: [security@verjson.com](mailto:security@verjson.com)

Please include as much of the following as you can:

- The type of issue (e.g. injection, authentication bypass, data exposure).
- The affected repository, version, or commit.
- Step-by-step instructions to reproduce the issue.
- Proof-of-concept or exploit code, if available.
- The impact of the issue and how an attacker might exploit it.

This information helps us triage and resolve the report more quickly.

## Our Commitment

When you report a vulnerability, we will:

1. **Acknowledge** receipt of your report within **3 business days**.
2. **Investigate** and provide an initial assessment of severity and validity.
3. **Keep you informed** of our progress toward a fix.
4. **Notify you** when the issue is resolved.

We ask that you give us a reasonable amount of time to address the issue before
any public disclosure, and that you make a good-faith effort to avoid privacy
violations, data destruction, and service interruption during your research.

## Supported Versions

Unless a specific repository states otherwise, security updates are provided for
the latest released version on the default branch. Individual projects may define
their own supported-version policy in their own `SECURITY.md`.

## Recognition

We're grateful to everyone who helps keep verJSON secure. With your permission,
we're happy to credit you for responsibly disclosed reports.
